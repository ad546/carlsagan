About

1934 - Birth on November 9th

1939 - Sagan goes to the New York World’s Fair which was “one of his most defining moments”

1951 - Graduated from high school

1954 - Earned BA degree in “nothing” from the University of Chicago

1955 - Earned BS degree in physics

1956 - Earned MS degree in physics

1960 - Earned PhD

1960 - 1962 - Was a Miller Fellow at the University of California, Berkeley

1963 - 1968 - Lectured at Harvard

1968 - Denied tenure at Harvard

1968 - Became a faculty member at Cornell

1972 - Assembled a plaque that was sent into space attached to Pioneer 10

1980 - Wrote Cosmos and produced Cosmos: A Personal Voyage

1985 - Wrote best selling science fiction novel Contact

1994 - Recipient of Public Welfare Medal

1994 - Wrote sequel to Cosmos named Pale Blue Dot

1996 - Death on December 20th

Cosmos

"If you wish to make an apple pie from scratch, you must first invent the universe."

“The nitrogen in our DNA, the calcium in our teeth, the iron in our blood, the carbon in our apple pies were made in the interiors of collapsing stars. We are made of starstuff.”

“Exploration is in our nature. We began as wanderers, and we are wanderers still. We have lingered long enough on the shores of the cosmic ocean. We are ready at last to set sail for the stars.”

- Carl Sagan

Cosmos is a popular (best selling scientific book until 1988) scientific non-fiction book written by Carl Sagan in 1980. It was Carl Sagan's way of helping those who wished to learn about the universe do so in an encouraging way. This book looks to relay facts about the universe, as known in 1980, to its audience while also imploring readers to imagine the possibilities of exploring the universe around them. Its popularity was helped by the making of Cosmos: A Personal Voyage, a tv show that aired around the same time as the publishing of this book.
